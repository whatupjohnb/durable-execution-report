The engineers building AI features are pulling more than their share of the
reliability load. Twenty percent of respondents at AI teams reported spending
up to half of their time keeping production workflows up — roughly double the
rate seen on non-AI teams.

The trend is directional, too. Year-over-year, the share of AI engineers
reporting an _increase_ in reliability work outpaces every other group.
Infrastructure that used to be stateless, idempotent, and fast-failing is
being replaced by orchestrated chains of model calls, tool invocations, and
long-running background jobs — each of which introduces a new failure mode
the team has to absorb.

<PullQuote attribution="Staff engineer, fintech, ~300 engineers">
  The AI team keeps promising "it's almost done." What they mean is: the model
  works. The pipeline around the model doesn't.
</PullQuote>

Across every segment of the survey, observability was the #1 problem
engineers believe is _still not solved._ This was true of AI teams and non-AI
teams alike, but the gap was sharpest for teams running multi-step workflows:
standard APM and tracing tooling, designed around a single request/response
unit, breaks down when the "request" is a 40-minute orchestration containing
twelve retries, three human-in-the-loop pauses, and two branching conditionals.

The teams reporting the most observability pain were — unsurprisingly — also
the teams spending the most time on reliability. The causal arrow runs both
ways: without first-class orchestration observability you can't debug fast,
and without fast debugging, reliability work expands to fill the vacuum.

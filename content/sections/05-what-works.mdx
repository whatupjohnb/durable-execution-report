When we segmented the 19% of teams who reported high confidence in scaling
their AI workflows, a consistent pattern emerged. These teams were
disproportionately likely to be running three things in combination — not
any one of them alone.

1. **Structured durable execution** at every model-call boundary.
2. **Orchestration-native observability** — traces that follow the workflow,
   not the HTTP request.
3. **Continuous production evals** — offline evaluation was correlated with
   confidence, but production evals were correlated with _scaling_ confidence
   specifically.

The inverse was also true: teams running only ad-hoc queueing (no durable
execution), logging-only observability, and no production evals were
disproportionately represented in the "not at all confident" segment.

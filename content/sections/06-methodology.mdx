This report is based on a structured survey of 130 engineers and engineering
directors conducted between January and March 2026. To qualify, respondents
needed to confirm that they personally run asynchronous work in production
today — this qualifying question is the reason the sample skews toward teams
who have already graduated past synchronous, request/response-only systems.

Respondents were recruited through Inngest's community channels, partner
organizations, and a cold-outbound list balanced for company size. We
de-duplicated multiple respondents from the same company and discarded any
response that couldn't verify their role.

Company-size buckets used throughout the report:

- **1–10** engineers (solopreneurs, early startups)
- **11–50** engineers (Series A / early growth)
- **51–200** engineers (Series B / scaling)
- **201–500** engineers (late-stage)
- **500+** engineers (public / established enterprise)

The word "AI team" in this report refers to teams that self-identified as
shipping at least one AI-powered feature to production users. It is
deliberately a broader definition than "teams working primarily on ML
research."

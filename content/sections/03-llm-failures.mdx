For the first time in the survey's history, a dependency on an external model
provider — rather than an internal infrastructure failure — is the most cited
cause of production workflow failure. 56% of teams building AI in production
name LLM or external API failures as a top cause of incidents, displacing
infrastructure crashes, which remain in the top slot for non-AI teams.

The shift matters because the standard playbook for infrastructure crashes
(retries, circuit breakers, autoscaling) does not cleanly translate to model
failures: a retried hallucination is still a hallucination, and rate-limit
backoff against a provider is fundamentally different from backoff against
your own services.

Teams that reported high confidence in scale were three times more likely to
be running structured **durable execution** around each model call — meaning
the call was checkpointed, idempotent, and retriable at the step level — and
correspondingly less exposed to this new failure mode.
